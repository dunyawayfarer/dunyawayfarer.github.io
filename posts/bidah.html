Understanding Bid'ah in Islam

[ 10-03-2025 ]

Bid'ah (innovation) in Islam refers to introducing new practices or beliefs into the religion that were not part of the original teachings of the Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫. The Prophet Ô∑∫ warned against Bid'ah, saying:

"Every innovation is misguidance, and every misguidance is in the Hellfire." (Sahih Muslim)

This hadith emphasizes the importance of adhering to the Quran and Sunnah and avoiding practices that deviate from them.

There are two types of Bid'ah:

  • Bid'ah Hasanah (Good Innovation): Some scholars argue that certain innovations can be good if they do not contradict Islamic principles. However, this is a minority opinion.
  • Bid'ah Sayyi'ah (Bad Innovation): Innovations that contradict the Quran and Sunnah are considered harmful and must be avoided.

It is crucial for Muslims to educate themselves about the teachings of Islam and avoid practices that are not supported by authentic sources.
